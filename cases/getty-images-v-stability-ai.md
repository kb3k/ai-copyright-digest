<h1 class="case-page-title">Getty Images v. Stability AI</h1>

This case involves 5 key legal issues related to AI copyright and training data usage.

<section class="legal-issues-section">
    <h2>Key Legal Issues</h2>


<details class="legal-issue unresolved">
<summary><strong>Copyright defenses and their limits, Precedential implications</strong></summary>

<div class="legal-issue-content">
    <p>The core question is whether Stability AI's use of Getty Images' photographs for training its AI model constitutes fair use under copyright law. The dispute centers on whether the AI's outputs can be considered transformative, with Getty arguing that the use is commercial and harms the market for its images, while Stability AI contends that the use is transformative and does not harm the market. <a href="#article-8" class="citation">[8]</a>, <a href="#article-11" class="citation">[11]</a>, <a href="#article-12" class="citation">[12]</a>, <a href="#article-18" class="citation">[18]</a></p>
    
</div>

</details>

<details class="legal-issue unresolved">
<summary><strong>Copyright defenses and their limits, Related legal theories</strong></summary>

<div class="legal-issue-content">
    <p>The question of whether Stability AI's inclusion of Getty Images' trademarks in AI-generated outputs constitutes trademark infringement remains unresolved. The court ruled that the outputs infringed trademarks, but the extent of liability for Stability AI and the users of the AI remains a point of contention. <a href="#article-19" class="citation">[19]</a>, <a href="#article-20" class="citation">[20]</a></p>
    
</div>

</details>

<details class="legal-issue unresolved">
<summary><strong>Copyright defenses and their limits, Related legal theories</strong></summary>

<div class="legal-issue-content">
    <p>The extent of database rights infringement claims against Stability AI is unclear. Getty Images alleges that Stability AI infringed its database rights by using its extensive collection of images without permission, but the court's ruling on this aspect remains ambiguous. <a href="#article-1" class="citation">[1]</a>, <a href="#article-11" class="citation">[11]</a>, <a href="#article-15" class="citation">[15]</a></p>
    
</div>

</details>

<details class="legal-issue unresolved">
<summary><strong>Economic/business model impacts on creators vs. AI companies, Precedential implications</strong></summary>

<div class="legal-issue-content">
    <p>The implications of the court's ruling on the economic model for creators versus AI companies are significant. The ruling may affect how AI companies license content and compensate creators, with Getty advocating for stronger protections for copyright owners in light of the decision. <a href="#article-9" class="citation">[9]</a>, <a href="#article-13" class="citation">[13]</a>, <a href="#article-18" class="citation">[18]</a></p>
    
</div>

</details>

<details class="legal-issue unresolved">
<summary><strong>Jurisdictional questions, Precedential implications</strong></summary>

<div class="legal-issue-content">
    <p>The court's decision raises questions about the jurisdictional reach of UK copyright law in relation to international AI companies. The applicability of UK laws to Stability AI, which operates globally, is a contested issue that could have broader implications for international copyright enforcement. <a href="#article-10" class="citation">[10]</a>, <a href="#article-12" class="citation">[12]</a>, <a href="#article-16" class="citation">[16]</a></p>
    
</div>

</details>

<section class="outcomes-section">
    <h2>Possible Outcomes</h2>
    
<details class="outcome-details">
<summary><strong>Plaintiff (Getty Images) wins</strong></summary>
<div class="outcome-content">
    If Getty Images prevails, the court's ruling could set a significant precedent on fair use limits in AI training. The key issue is whether Stability AI's use of Getty's images is transformative; Getty claims Stability's commercial use harms its market, while Stability argues the AI outputs are transformative [8, 10]. A ruling for Getty could result in millions in damages for the unauthorized use of over 12 million images [9, 11]. This may force AI companies to adopt stricter licensing agreements and operational changes, affecting their economic models and creator compensation [13, 19]. Additionally, the court's decision on trademark infringement could clarify AI developers' responsibilities regarding user-generated content, potentially increasing operational burdens [19, 20]. However, the ruling would not address broader issues of international copyright enforcement and the jurisdictional reach of UK law over global AI operations, leaving these questions for future litigation [10, 12].
</div>
</details>

<details class="outcome-details">
<summary><strong>Defendant (Stability AI) wins</strong></summary>
<div class="outcome-content">
    If Stability AI wins, it would mark a significant legal victory for AI developers, supporting the notion that AI training can be considered fair use, especially if outputs are transformative [10, 12]. This could weaken Getty's market power, allowing AI companies to use large amounts of copyrighted material without permission, potentially devaluing creative works [9, 19]. The court's ruling on trademark infringement found Stability liable but limited user responsibility, prompting AI companies to adopt stricter controls over training data [19, 20]. However, questions remain about fair compensation for creators and compliance with international copyright laws [10, 12]. The ruling would not clarify database rights infringement claims, leaving creators uncertain about their protections in the evolving AI landscape [1, 11].
</div>
</details>
